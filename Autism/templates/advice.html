What is Autism?
Autism is a neurodevelopmental disorder that affects communication, social interaction, and behavior. Symptoms typically appear in the first two years of life and can range from mild to severe. Autistic have difficulties with social interaction and communication, such as making eye contact, initiating and sustaining conversations, and understanding nonverbal cues. They may also exhibit repetitive behaviors or routines, have narrow interests, and have difficulty with changes in their environment.
What are types of autism ?
1. Asperger’s Syndrome
2. Rett Syndrome
3. Childhood Disintegrative
4. Kanner’s Syndrome
5. Pervasive Developmental
Asperger’s Syndrome
  • Avoid eye contact.
  • Weakness in social interaction.
  • Difficulty understanding nonverbal gestures, such as facial expressions.
  • Tendency to repeat the same behaviors and activities.
  • Taking speech literally and not understanding sarcasm or humor.
Rett Syndrome
  • Loss of standard movement and Coordination
  • Loss of communication and speech.
  • Breathing difficulties.
Childhood Disintegrative
    The child may lose any of the following skills and abilities:
  • Toileting skills if they had already been established.
  • Acquired language or vocabularies.
  • Social skills and adaptive behaviors.
  • Some motor skills.
Kanner’s Syndrome
  • Lack of emotional attachment.
  • Lack of Communication and interaction challenges.
  • Uncontrolled speech.
  • Obsession with handling objects.
  • A high degree of rote memory and visuospatial skills with major difficulties learning in other areas.
Pervasive Developmental
  • challenges in social and language.
  • You can identify this type of autism by observing the child and noting what area the child displays a deficit in, such as interacting.
  • Your child may experience delays in language development.
  • Your child may experience delays in walking, and other motor skills.
Is Autism inherited ?
  • Studies have shown that autism is predominantly a genetic disorder, with 40-80% heritability.
  • Researchers are still working to identify all of the genes associated with autism, they do know that some genetic mutations can increase your risk of developing autism.
  • If you have a family member with autism, you may want to consider talking to your doctor about getting screened for genetic mutations that could put you at higher risk.
  • Children in families with a history of other mental conditions like ADHD, schizophrenia, and bipolar disorder are also at a higher risk for developing autism.
  • Children who have a first-degree relative — a sibling or parent — with a brain condition other than autism have up to 4.7 times the odds of having autism and up to 7.6 times the odds of having both autism and intellectual disability.
What is Lovas?
  • The Lovas program, known as the Lovaas method or ABA therapy, is a type of behavioral therapy used to treat autism.
  • It was developed by Dr. Ivar Lovaas in the 1960s and is based on the principles of applied behavior analysis (ABA).
  • The therapy involves using positive reinforcement to teach individuals with autism a wide range of skills, including communication, social interaction, self-care, and academics.
  • The therapy is typically provided in one-on-one sessions and is tailored to the individual's specific needs and abilities.
  • The therapy is widely considered as an evidence-based practice but also receive some critics for being intensive, costly, and not suitable for all individuals with autism.
